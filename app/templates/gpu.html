GPU (Graphical Processing Unit)

Description

The Graphical Processing Unit or GPU, is an expansion card used to generate a feed of output images to a monitor or other display device. GPU's are capable of many different tasks from playing video games to deep learning. The GPU is able to break complex problems into millions of seperate tasks which are all processed at the same time, which makes the GPU ideal for things like shiny graphics, as textures and the rendering of shapes have to all be processed at the same time in order to keep images flying across the screen.

Installation

    All you need:
  • A graphics card
  • A PC
  • Phillips-head screwdriver

    • Steps:
  • Unscrew the retention bracket on the rear interior of your PC case.
  • Place the GPU into the PCIe x16 slot of your motherboard.
  • Plug in the PCIe power cables from your power supply into your GPU.
  • Screw the rentention bracket from before back on to the rear interior of your PC case so that your GPU can sit there firmly.
  • Boot into your chosen OS and install your respective graphic card drivers.

Recommendation

Depending on your computer needs, there is a lot to consider when picking GPU, gamers usually want the most frames and pretty graphics, so they will typically buy the standard gaming focused graphics cards, those currently being RTX or RX series from Nvidia and AMD respectivly. However professionals in specfic fields such as computer aided design (CAD), computer-generated imagery(CGI) or digital content creation(DCC) will often go for the Quadro or Radeon Pro series line of graphics cards from Nvidia and AMD respectively, as those cards were designed with those specific tasks in mind for workstations.

RAM (Random Access Memory)

Description

Random Access Memory or RAM, is a type of volatile computer memory which stores information that your computer is actively using so it can accessed very quickly. It is one of most important components when it comes to computer performance because too little of it can make your computer feel sluggish however, too much of it may feel completely unnecessary, but all of this is dependant on your system use.

Installation

To install RAM, you must place it into the corresponding DIMM slots of your motherboard, in order to get the full performance out of your RAM, you should always consult your motherboard's manual, on the optimized placement of your RAM, for example in the most up to date motherboards, if you had two sticks of RAM and four DIMM slots, you would place the RAM sticks in the 1st and 3rd DIMM slots as the first two slots share the same channel leading to reduced performance.

Recommendation

Depending on your computer needs, there is a lot to consider when picking RAM, are you a gamer? then 16GB's of high speed low latency RAM should be more than enough for high FPS gaming, on the otherhand, you might a professional video editor and you are dealing with lots of big video files all while rendering another video, then maybe 32GB or 64GB's of RAM might suit your needs.
